Frequently Asked Questions
Find quick answers to the most common questions about our services
Who is eligible to apply for benefits under PCR and PoA Acts?
Victims of caste-based atrocities, couples in inter-caste marriages, and their family members are eligible. You must be an Indian citizen with valid Aadhaar and supporting documentation such as FIR, court orders, or marriage certificate.
What documents do I need to apply?
Required documents include: Aadhaar card, bank account details with canceled cheque, FIR copy (if applicable), caste certificate, marriage certificate (for inter-caste marriage incentive), income certificate, and any medical or legal documents supporting your case.
How long does the application process take?
The typical processing time is 15-30 working days from the date of submission. However, this may vary based on document verification, case complexity, and fund availability. You can track your application status in real-time through our portal.
How will I receive the funds?
Funds are transferred directly to your bank account through the Public Financial Management System (PFMS). You will receive SMS and email notifications at each stage, including when the funds are disbursed.
Can I edit my application after submission?
Once submitted, applications cannot be edited directly. However, you can submit additional documents or corrections through the grievance portal. Contact our support team for assistance with urgent changes.
What should I do if my application is rejected?
If your application is rejected, you will receive detailed reasons via SMS and email. You can file a grievance through our portal or reapply with corrected/additional documents. Our support team can guide you through the reapplication process.
Is my personal information secure?
Yes, we use end-to-end encryption for all data transmission and storage. Your information is processed as per the Data Protection Act and is only used for verification and benefit disbursement purposes. We never share your data with unauthorized third parties.
How can I check the status of my grievance?
You can track your grievance using the Grievance ID provided via SMS and email. Visit the Grievance Redressal page and enter your ID to view the current status, responses, and resolution timeline.
